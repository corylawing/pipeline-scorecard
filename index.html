Pipeline Problem

In EMEA, sellers with more opportunities have more stalled deals, not fewer. Sellers carrying 30+ opps have 70–90% of their pipe stalled. Sellers with 10–25 opps have 0–50% stalled. The 30–50 opps KPI is making the problem worse — sellers are stuffing pipeline to hit the count, and the extra opps just sit. How a seller splits their time between in-person, phone, and virtual doesn't change this. Fix: drop the flat opp-count target. Replace it with a coverage target tailored to each seller's win rate, deal size, and cycle time.

For Sales Directors — the one line

EMEA has closed €971K YTD across 117 wins — but 73% of our €7.5M open pipeline is stalled, meaning real H2-workable pipe is closer to €2M, not €7.5M. The QBR conversation isn't who's closing; it's whether we triage the €5M of stale inventory or accept a much lower forecast.

Source: Salesforce, May 11, 2026. Active sellers only (Owner.IsActive = true). Closed won and open pipe figures cover 2026 YTD. Avg cycle and close-won rate use combined 2025+2026 closures plus current open opps. Currency converted to EUR at mid-market rates (not finance-grade).

Acquisition (Acq) = New Practice + Reactivate Practice opportunity types. Acq stalled = open Acq opportunity with no stage change in 60+ days. For reference, 90% of NP wins close in under 35 days. The aging strip splits ALL open opps into 0–30d (fresh), 30–60d (warming), 60–90d (stalled), 90d+ (severely stalled). Stage bar shows Discover → Consult → Close → Order Form Sent → Order Form Viewed.

Avg cycle = mean days between CreatedDate and CloseDate for combined 2025+2026 closed-won opportunities. Close-won rate = won / (won + lost + open) across all the seller's opportunities. Open opportunities count against the rate because they haven't converted yet — when they do close, they shift the rate accordingly. This makes pipe hygiene (advancing or killing dead deals) directly visible in the seller's number. Suspiciously short cycles (under 10d) combined with high close-won rates indicate retroactive opportunity creation — chronic for Sascha Derichs.
